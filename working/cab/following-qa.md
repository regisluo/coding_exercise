## **2 Strategic Questions to Ask the Interviewer**

*These questions demonstrate your seniority, strategic thinking, and genuine interest while gathering critical
information for your decision-making.*

---

## **Question 1: Understanding the Project & Strategic Context**

### **The Question:**

*"Can you walk me through the current state of your AFC technology platform and the key challenges or initiatives you're
looking to address in the next 12-18 months? I'm particularly interested in understanding:*

- *What are the biggest pain points—whether that's scalability, false positive rates, regulatory gaps, or legacy system
  limitations?*
- *Are you modernizing existing systems, building new capabilities, or both?*
- *How does this role fit into that roadmap—am I leading the technical transformation, enhancing existing platforms, or
  building something new?*
- *What does success look like for this role in the first year?"*

---

### **Why This Question Works:**

**1. Shows Strategic Thinking:**

- You're not asking "what will I do day-to-day" (junior question)
- You're asking about the bigger picture and how you fit in (senior leadership question)
- Demonstrates you think in terms of outcomes and impact, not just tasks

**2. Reveals Critical Information:**

**About the Project:**

```
Their Answer Will Tell You:

Modernization Journey:
├── "Legacy rules engine, needs real-time capability" 
│   → You'll be transforming architecture (exciting, high-impact)
├── "Recently implemented new platform, need optimization"
│   → You'll be enhancing/scaling (lower risk, incremental improvement)
└── "Building greenfield ML-based system"
    → You'll be architecting from scratch (high autonomy, high risk)

Pain Points:
├── "False positive rate is killing us" 
│   → ML/data science focus needed
├── "Can't scale for transaction growth"
│   → Infrastructure/performance optimization focus
├── "Regulatory audit findings to address"
│   → Compliance/data quality focus
└── "Analyst tools are terrible"
    → UX/workflow improvement focus

Timeline & Expectations:
├── "Need quick wins in Q1" → High pressure, show value fast
├── "Long-term strategic transformation" → Patient, multi-year investment
└── "Both quick wins AND strategic" → Balanced approach (best scenario)
```

**3. Helps You Assess Fit:**

```
Red Flags to Listen For:
├── ❌ Vague answers ("we're exploring options")
│   → They don't have a clear strategy
├── ❌ Unrealistic expectations ("modernize everything in 6 months")
│   → Setting you up for failure
├── ❌ Conflicting priorities ("do everything at once")
│   → Lack of focus, scope thrash
└── ❌ "Previous tech lead failed to deliver"
    → Understand WHY before accepting

Green Flags:
├── ✅ Clear pain points and priorities
├── ✅ Realistic timeline and phased approach
├── ✅ Executive sponsorship mentioned
├── ✅ Investment in team and technology
└── ✅ Success metrics defined
```

**4. Positions You as a Problem-Solver:**

- You're already thinking about how to address their challenges
- Shows you've thought about AFC domain (even if you're learning)
- Demonstrates you want to make an impact, not just do a job

---

### **Follow-Up Questions (Based on Their Answer):**

**If They Mention Legacy Modernization:**

- *"What's driving the modernization—regulatory pressure, business growth, cost optimization, or all three?"*
- *"What constraints do I need to work within—existing vendor relationships, budget limits, team skills?"*

**If They Mention False Positives:**

- *"What's the current false positive rate, and what's the target? Is there buy-in for ML approaches from compliance and
  regulators?"*

**If They Mention Scalability:**

- *"What's the current transaction volume, and what growth are you planning for? Are there specific peak periods (
  month-end, etc.) that are challenging?"*

**If They Mention Regulatory Gaps:**

- *"Are there specific AUSTRAC findings or audit gaps we're addressing? How involved will I be with regulators
  directly?"*

## **Question 2: Understanding the Team & Operating Environment**

### **The Question:**

*"Can you tell me about the team I'd be leading or working with? Specifically:*

- *What's the current team structure—how many engineers, data scientists, analysts, and what are their experience
  levels?*
- *What's the team's current capability in modern technologies like cloud platforms, real-time streaming, and ML—are we
  building skills or hiring?*
- *How does this team collaborate with other stakeholders—compliance, data science, operations, infrastructure? Who are
  my key partners?*
- *What's the team culture like—how do you approach innovation vs risk, autonomy vs governance, and work-life balance?*
- *Are there any known team challenges I should be aware of—skill gaps, morale issues, turnover, or recent changes?"*

---

### **Why This Question Works:**

**1. Shows Leadership Mindset:**

- You're thinking about the people, not just the technology
- Demonstrates you understand delivery happens through teams
- Shows you'll invest in team development (not just personal contribution)

**2. Reveals Team Reality:**

```
Their Answer Will Tell You:

Team Composition:
├── "3 senior engineers, 5 mid-level, 2 grads"
│   → Strong team, can delegate and focus on strategy
├── "2 engineers, but we're hiring 5 more"
│   → Heavy hiring/onboarding load initially
└── "Team of 15, but mostly contractors"
    → Retention risk, knowledge continuity challenges

Skill Levels:
├── "Strong in traditional tech, learning cloud/ML"
│   → Upskilling needed (opportunity to mentor)
├── "Very experienced, self-sufficient"
│   → Less hands-on needed, more strategic focus
└── "Junior team, need strong technical leadership"
    → Heavy mentoring load (assess if you enjoy this)

Collaboration Model:
├── "Embedded in AFC business unit, daily contact with compliance"
│   → Close business partnership (good for impact)
├── "Centralized tech team, serve multiple business units"
│   → Competing priorities, stakeholder management critical
└── "Cross-functional squads with compliance, data science, ops"
    → Agile model, need strong collaboration skills

Culture:
├── "Innovation-driven, empowered teams"
│   → Autonomy, experimentation (exciting)
├── "Risk-averse, governance-heavy"
│   → Process-oriented, slower (frustrating if you like speed)
└── "Balanced—innovate within guardrails"
    → Best scenario for AFC (innovation + compliance)
```

**3. Uncovers Team Challenges:**

```
Red Flags:
├── ❌ High recent turnover ("3 engineers left in 6 months")
│   → Morale issues, culture problems, or poor leadership
├── ❌ "Previous tech lead struggled with the team"
│   → Difficult team dynamics or unrealistic expectations
├── ❌ Vague about team ("we're figuring out the structure")
│   → You'll be building from scratch (high effort)
├── ❌ "Very siloed, need someone to break down barriers"
│   → Political challenges, resistance to change
└── ❌ "Team is burned out from regulatory project"
    → Morale recovery needed before pushing hard

Green Flags:
├── ✅ Stable team with growth opportunities
├── ✅ Clear roles and responsibilities
├── ✅ Strong collaboration with stakeholders
├── ✅ Invested in learning and development
├── ✅ Recent successes to build on
└── ✅ Excited about new leadership (you)
```

**4. Helps You Assess Cultural Fit:**

```
Work Environment Questions to Probe:
├── Remote/hybrid/office expectations?
├── On-call responsibilities?
├── Work-life balance norms (regular hours vs "always on")?
├── Decision-making: Top-down vs collaborative?
└── Failure tolerance: Punitive vs learning culture?

Your Preference (Based on Your Experience):
├── ✅ Collaborative, transparent culture
├── ✅ Empowered teams with accountability
├── ✅ Learning from failures (blameless post-mortems)
├── ✅ Balanced work-life (sustainable pace)
└── ✅ Innovation within governance (not wild west)
```

---

### **Follow-Up Questions (Based on Their Answer):**

**If They Mention Skill Gaps:**

- *"What's the budget and timeline for upskilling or hiring? Do I have autonomy in team growth decisions?"*

**If They Mention High Turnover:**

- *"What's been done to address the root causes? What would you like me to do differently as the new leader?"*

**If They Mention Collaboration Challenges:**

- *"Can you give me an example of a recent collaboration challenge and how it was resolved (or not)?"*

**If They Mention Regulatory Pressure:**

- *"How does the team currently handle the pressure—do they see it as burden or opportunity? How can I support them?"*

**If Team Sounds Strong:**

- *"What keeps this strong team engaged? What would make them even more effective?"*

## **Bonus: How to Ask These Questions**

### **Tone & Approach:**

**Do:**

```
✅ Frame as genuine curiosity: "I'm trying to understand..."
✅ Show you've thought about it: "In my experience... so I'm curious how you..."
✅ Be conversational, not interrogational
✅ Listen actively, don't just wait for your turn to ask next question
✅ Build on their answers: "That's interesting, because..."
```

**Don't:**

```
❌ Sound like you're interviewing them (power dynamic shift)
❌ Interrogate with rapid-fire questions
❌ Challenge their answers defensively
❌ Ask yes/no questions (ask open-ended)
❌ Only ask about what benefits you (show team interest too)
```

---

### **When to Ask:**

**Timing Strategy:**

```
Early Interview (If Invited):
├── "Before we dive into my background, can I ask a quick question 
│    about the role to make sure I'm focusing on the most relevant 
│    parts of my experience?"
└── Shows strategic thinking, helps you tailor your answers

Mid-Interview (Natural Break):
├── After they ask about your experience with similar projects
├── "That reminds me—can you share more about the specific 
│    initiative I'd be working on?"
└── Conversational, builds on discussion

End Interview (When They Ask "Questions for Us?"):
├── Standard time for candidate questions
├── Ask both questions, taking notes
└── Shows you're serious about decision-making
```
